# Corrective Drills & Practice Suggestions

This document maps common squash errors to specific corrective drills. The AI coach can suggest these drills to players after identifying errors during video analysis.

---

## Footwork Drills

### Drill F1: Ghosting Circuit (For: No T-Recovery, Slow Recovery)
**Target errors**: B1 (No T-Recovery), I4 (Slow T-Recovery), A4 (Fatigue-Induced Breakdown)

**How to perform:**
1. Start on the T in ready position
2. Move to one of the 6 court positions (front-left, front-right, mid-left, mid-right, back-left, back-right)
3. Simulate a shot (shadow swing) with proper footwork and racket prep
4. Push back to the T immediately
5. Split-step on the T, then move to the next position

**Duration**: 3 sets of 2 minutes, 30 seconds rest between sets.

**Key focus**: The push-back to the T must be immediate and explosive — no walking, no pausing after the simulated shot.

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Try ghosting drills — 2 minutes of moving to each corner and sprinting back to the T. It builds the habit of automatic recovery."

---

### Drill F2: Split-Step Timer (For: No Split-Step, Late Split-Step)
**Target errors**: B3 (Flat-Footed), I1 (Lazy Split-Step)

**How to perform:**
1. Stand on the T with a partner on the other side
2. Partner hits random drives from the back (straight and crosscourt)
3. For each shot, perform a deliberate split-step just before the partner makes contact
4. Focus on TIMING, not the return — the goal is to land the split-step as the partner swings

**Duration**: 20 balls per set, 3 sets

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Practice your split-step timing — have a partner hit random shots while you focus on bouncing right as they swing. It should become automatic."

---

### Drill F3: Lunge Walks (For: Poor Lunge Technique)
**Target errors**: B3 (Flat-Footed at the ball), general mobility

**How to perform:**
1. Walk the length of the court performing alternating lunges
2. Each lunge: front knee at 90°, back knee nearly touching the floor
3. Hold each lunge for 2 seconds at the bottom
4. Push up explosively to stand, then step into the next lunge

**Duration**: 3 sets of 10 lunges per leg

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Strengthen your lunges with lunge walks — focus on getting low with the front knee at 90 degrees and pushing up explosively."

## Racket Technique Drills

### Drill R1: Wall Rally with Prep Focus (For: Late Racket Preparation)
**Target errors**: B2 (No Racket Prep), A1 (Late Prep Under Pressure)

**How to perform:**
1. Stand 2 meters from the front wall
2. Hit forehand drives to yourself continuously
3. After EACH shot, immediately bring the racket back up to the ready position (shoulder height)
4. Do not let the racket drop below waist level at any point
5. Repeat on backhand side

**Duration**: 50 consecutive shots forehand, 50 backhand, 3 sets

**Key focus**: The racket must be back in ready position BEFORE the ball returns from the front wall. If you're scrambling to prepare, slow down the pace.

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Solo drill against the front wall — hit drives and force yourself to bring the racket back up immediately after each shot. The racket must be ready before the ball comes back."

---

### Drill R2: Shadow Swings in Mirror (For: Swing Path, Follow-Through)
**Target errors**: B2, general technique refinement

**How to perform:**
1. Stand in front of a mirror (or use phone camera for self-review)
2. Perform slow-motion forehand and backhand swings with no ball
3. Check: racket starts high, drops through contact zone, finishes high over the opposite shoulder
4. Repeat at gradually increasing speed

**Duration**: 20 slow swings per side, 20 medium speed, 20 full speed

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Practice your swing in slow motion — start with the racket high, swing through, and finish high over your opposite shoulder. Use a mirror or phone to check your form."

---

### Drill R3: Spacing Drill — Cone/Marker (For: Crowding)
**Target errors**: B4 (Crowding), I7 (Inconsistent Spacing)

**How to perform:**
1. Place a marker (cone, water bottle) on the court where the ball typically bounces
2. Practice approaching the marker and setting up at arm's-length distance
3. Simulate a swing — the racket should comfortably extend to the marker without the arm being jammed
4. Repeat from different angles (front, side, back approach)

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "To fix crowding, practice approaching a marker on the floor and stopping at arm's-length distance. Your racket should reach the spot comfortably without your elbow touching your body."

## Shot Selection Drills

### Drill S1: Straight Drive Target (For: Hitting to the Middle)
**Target errors**: B5 (Hitting to the Middle), I2 (Crosscourt from Back)

**How to perform:**
1. Place a target on the side wall at the back of the court (e.g., tape or marker at the service box line, 30 cm from the back corner)
2. Stand at mid-court and hit straight drives aiming to hit the side wall target
3. Focus on keeping the ball tight to the wall — the ball should pass within 30 cm of the side wall

**Duration**: 30 balls per side, 3 sets

**Key focus**: Quality over quantity. If the ball comes more than 50 cm from the side wall, it counts as a miss.

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Practice straight drives with a target on the side wall — aim to keep every ball within 30 centimeters of the wall. It's the most important shot in squash."

---

### Drill S2: Drop Shot Touch (For: Over-Hitting Drops)
**Target errors**: I3 (Over-Hitting Drops)

**How to perform:**
1. Stand at the front of the court near the T
2. Feed yourself a ball (bounce it or toss it up)
3. Play a straight drop shot aiming to have the ball die before the short line
4. Focus on soft touch — the ball should barely clear the tin and the second bounce should be before the short line

**Duration**: 20 drops per side, 3 sets

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Work on your drop shot touch — feed yourself balls at the front and practice making the ball die just above the tin. Focus on softness and precision, not power."

---

### Drill S3: Volley Practice — T Intercept (For: Not Volleying)
**Target errors**: I6 (Not Volleying When Possible)

**How to perform:**
1. Partner hits drives from the back of the court
2. Stand near the T and volley EVERY ball that passes through your reach zone
3. Aim volley drives deep to the back corner
4. Focus on compact swing (not big backswing) and timing

**Duration**: 30 volleys, 3 sets. Partner alternates sides.

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Practice volleying from the T — have a partner drive balls from the back while you intercept everything in reach. Use a compact swing and aim deep."

## Tactical Drills

### Drill T1: Condition Game — Length Only (For: Patience, Building Pressure)
**Target errors**: I5 (Telegraphing), A3 (Unnecessary Risk-Taking)

**How to perform:**
1. Play a conditioned game where BOTH players can ONLY hit straight drives
2. The ball must pass the short line to be valid
3. Play first to 11 points — a point is won only if the opponent's ball is short of the short line or hits the tin

**Purpose**: Builds patience, groove straight drive technique, and emphasizes length as the foundation of squash.

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Play a conditioned game with your partner — straight drives only, past the short line. It teaches patience and builds rock-solid length."

---

### Drill T2: Boast-Drive Rotation (For: Court Coverage, Tactical Variation)
**Target errors**: B1 (No T-Recovery), A5 (Over-Reliance on Favorite Shots)

**How to perform:**
1. Player A plays a boast from the back corner
2. Player B plays a straight drive from the front
3. Player A covers the straight drive and plays another boast
4. Continue the rotation, then switch roles

**Purpose**: Practices full-court movement (front-to-back), boast technique, and straight drive accuracy from the front under movement.

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Try the boast-drive drill with a partner — one boasts from the back, the other drives from the front. Great for court coverage and movement patterns."

---

### Drill T3: Serve + 1 Practice (For: Serve Strategy)
**Target errors**: General serve improvement

**How to perform:**
1. Practice serving (lob serve) to the back corner
2. After serving, move immediately to the T
3. Partner returns the serve — you play ONE aggressive shot (volley, drive, or drop)
4. Stop the rally after the first shot

**Purpose**: Builds the habit of moving to the T after serving and capitalizing on the server's positional advantage.

**Coaching cue to suggest:** "Practice 'serve + 1' — serve deep, sprint to the T, and play one attacking shot. It teaches you to take control right from the serve."

## Mental / Visualization Cues

These short mental cues can be included in coaching tips to help players internalize correct patterns:

| Concept | Mental Cue |
|---|---|
| T-Recovery | "Imagine a rubber band attached to your waist pulling you back to the T" |
| Split-Step | "Think of it as loading a spring — small bounce, big reaction" |
| Racket Prep | "Racket up before your feet move — prepare then run" |
| Spacing | "Stand next to the ball, not on top of it" |
| Length | "Paint the side wall with your drives" |
| Defensive Lob | "When in doubt, go UP — height is your best friend" |
| Patience | "Win the back wall before going to the front" |
| Volleying | "If you can touch it, volley it — don't let it pass" |
